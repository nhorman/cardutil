Coincidental Death?

Thomas Jefferson, and John Adams, bothers in arms during the revolution, found themselves on opposite sides of the Republican/Federalist divide during the early years of the United States, and reconciled when Jefferson sent Adams a new years greeting on Jan 1, 1812.  This started a 15 year letter exchange between the two men in which they rekindled their friendship, until their almost tandem deaths on July 4, 1826.
