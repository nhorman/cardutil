Miss Unsinkable

$ANSWER1 had an adventurous life that she didn't particularly ask for.  In an effort to take care of her ailing mother, she took a job as a stewardess on the White Star line of ships in England in 1911, serving initially on the $ANSWER2.  In September of that year when the ship collided with the HMS Hawke near the Isle of Wight, sinking.  $ANSWER1 saved several lives during that sinking, and then went on to serve on an other White Star owned ship....the $ANSWER3.  She was on its maiden voyage in 1912, making a transatlantic crossing, when the ship famously collided with an iceberg, sinking.  Many lives were lost, but not $ANSWER1's.  She managed to help several women and children into lifeboats, including herself.

Not content with two sinkings, $ANSWER1 decided to enter the nursing field, and took a position on the $ANSWER4 with the British Red Cross.  In 1916 the $ANSWER4 struck a German mine in the Aegean Sea.  The ship sunk, but not $ANSWER1, who again survived, and saved many others with her. 

$ANSWER1 went on to continue working on cruise ships spanning a 42 year career at sea.
